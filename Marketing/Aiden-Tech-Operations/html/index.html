02 · Three Foundations

Graph. Inference. Agents. One system.

Not three products bolted together. A coherent operating architecture where memory, reasoning, and execution work as one — governed by explicit confidence and policy.

01 — KNOWLEDGE GRAPH

Operational memory for the enterprise

Partners, transaction patterns, mappings, incidents, remediations, SLAs, certificates, dependencies, and configurations — connected into one continuously evolving intelligence layer. Every signal understood in context, not as an isolated event.

02 — INFERENCE MODELS

Reasoning, not static rules

Inference models interpret real-time signals to identify risks, predict SLA breaches, recognize recurring patterns, and recommend corrective action. Reasoning runs across historical patterns, current state, partner criticality, and business impact.

03 — DYNAMIC AGENTS

Execution with guardrails

Agents monitor, reason, and act. Work is prioritised by severity, confidence, business impact, partner tier, and policy threshold — not ticket arrival order. Routine events resolved autonomously; complex cases routed to the right tier of human oversight.

03 · Runbooks, Rebuilt

Runbooks as executable patterns, not wiki pages.

Traditional runbooks decay in wikis. Aiden transforms them into intelligent, executable operational patterns. Agents use them to identify known issues, determine the correct resolution path, perform checks in sequence, apply approved remediations, and validate outcomes. Operational knowledge becomes a reusable, governed, versioned asset.

04 · Governed Escalation

Each tier, its real job.

Governable L1–L3 escalation where each tier has a clear scope defined by capability, not urgency. Ticket volume at lower tiers drops. L3 stops absorbing L1 work.

TIERL1

Autonomous execution

Agents handle repetitive, low-complexity events — known transaction failures, standard retries, routing issues, duplicate events, predefined validation breaches. Runbook-guided and inference-informed. Humans stay out of the loop by design, within policy.

AGENT-LED
TIERL2

Human-supervised exceptions

Operators supervise exceptions, review low-confidence decisions, validate non-standard remediation paths, and manage policy-sensitive events. The knowledge graph surfaces every relevant piece of context in one place.

HUMAN + AGENT
TIERL3

Engineering and platform specialists

Deep-rooted platform defects, architectural issues, unknown patterns, and design-level changes. L3 stops being a dumping ground for L1 problems and goes back to its actual job: resilience, platform improvement, future-state optimization.
