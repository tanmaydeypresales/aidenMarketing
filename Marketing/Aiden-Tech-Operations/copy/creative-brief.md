# Creative Brief — Aiden Tech Operations for B2B

**Objective**
Position Aiden Tech Operations as the autonomous operating model for B2B partner ecosystems — and generate qualified conversations with operations and platform leaders drowning in ticket-driven support.

**Primary audience**
- VP / Head of B2B Operations, Integration Operations
- Head of Application / Production Support
- Director of Platform Engineering, SRE Lead
- CIO / CTO
- Head of Shared Services for B2B

**Funnel stage**
Consideration → Evaluation. These buyers already know their ops model is the bottleneck. The copy has to prove this is a governed operating shift, not another AIOps dashboard.

**Pain points**
- Ticket-driven triage absorbs senior engineers who should be building resilience
- Runbooks live in wikis and tribal memory; quality depends on who picks up the ticket
- SLA breaches detected after the fact, not predicted
- Monitoring tells you what broke, not why, not what to do, not who to involve
- Certificate expiries, config drift, and duplicate events keep happening for the same reasons
- L2 and L3 teams spend most of their cycles on L1-class work

**Desired action**
Book a 30-minute walkthrough with an Aiden solution architect using a real operational pattern — stuck transactions, recurring mapping defect, certificate auto-rotation, or SLA breach scenario.

**Value proposition**
An AI-native operating model built on a semantic knowledge graph, inference models, and dynamic agents — with executable runbooks and a governed L1–L3 escalation framework — that moves B2B operations from reactive support to predictive, autonomous execution.

**Differentiators**
- Semantic knowledge graph as operational memory — partners, transactions, mappings, incidents, remediations, SLAs, certificates all connected
- Inference models that reason across historical patterns + current state + business impact, not static alert rules
- Dynamic agents with confidence scoring and policy guardrails
- Runbooks as executable operational patterns, not static docs
- Governed L1–L3 escalation with measurable autonomy thresholds
- Three-layer operating model: Autonomous Execution Fabric · Human Governance · Strategic Optimization

**Proof points (indicative)**
- Reduction in L1 ticket volume on repetitive event classes
- MTTR compression through runbook-guided remediation
- Senior engineer cycles recovered for engineering and optimization work
- Predictive SLA breach detection ahead of impact

**Tone**
Executive, operations-literate, engineering-credible. Use real ops vocabulary (MTTR, runbook, autonomy threshold, confidence score, escalation tier, certificate rotation). No AIOps hype. Acknowledge governance and human oversight explicitly.

**Channels**
Landing page, LinkedIn feed + carousel, executive email, one-pager leave-behind, blog article, whitepaper, sales enablement.

**Primary CTA:** Book a 30-minute Tech Operations walkthrough
**Secondary CTA:** Download the whitepaper

**Assumptions**
- No client references in public copy; outcomes described as indicative
- Global campaign, no region variants in v1
- Whitepaper serves as gated MQL asset
