## Headline

## Sub-headline
An AI-native operating model for B2B partner ecosystems — semantic knowledge graph, inference models, dynamic agents, executable runbooks, governed L1–L3 escalation.

## The problem (3 bullets)
- Senior engineers burn their days triaging L1-class tickets
- Runbooks live in wikis and tribal memory; resolution depends on who picks up
- Monitoring tells you what broke, not why or what to do

## The operating model (6 pillars)

**1. Semantic knowledge graph** — partners, transactions, mappings, incidents, SLAs, certificates, configs, remediations — all connected as operational memory.

**2. Inference models** — reason across historical patterns, current state, partner tier, and business impact. Predict SLA breaches ahead of impact.

**3. Dynamic agents** — monitor, reason, and act. Prioritised by confidence, severity, and policy — not ticket arrival order.

**4. Executable runbooks** — operational knowledge as code. Identify, check, remediate, validate. Governed and versioned.

**5. Governed L1–L3 escalation** — agents clear L1 within policy, humans supervise L2 exceptions, engineering owns L3 design-level work only.

**6. Three-layer future model** — Autonomous Execution Fabric · Human Governance · Strategic Optimization.
