## The problem — your best engineers are triaging tickets
In most B2B ecosystems, operations teams spend their days managing partner transactions, mapping issues, connectivity failures, SLA risks, certificate expiries, exceptions, and recurring defects across a highly interdependent landscape. Monitoring dashboards tell you what broke. Fragmented runbooks and tribal knowledge tell you what *might* work. Escalation-heavy support queues pull your most experienced engineers into repetitive troubleshooting rather than resilience, engineering, and optimization.

The business cost doesn't sit in the ticket count — it sits in what your senior people aren't doing because they're clearing it.

## The shift — from reactive support to autonomous operating model
Aiden Tech Operations is not another AIOps dashboard bolted onto existing tools. It's an operating model built on three foundations that work as one:

### 1. Semantic knowledge graph — operational memory for the enterprise
Partners, transaction patterns, mappings, interfaces, incidents, remediation histories, SLA commitments, certificates, dependencies, configurations, and prior resolutions — all connected into a structured, continuously evolving intelligence layer. Every operational signal is understood in context, not as an isolated event.

### 2. Inference models — reasoning across signals, not static rules
Inference models continuously interpret real-time signals and operational context to identify emerging risks, detect anomalies, predict SLA breaches, recognize recurring patterns, and recommend or trigger corrective actions. The system reasons across historical patterns, current transaction state, partner criticality, and business impact.

### 3. Dynamic agents — execution with guardrails
Agents solve specific operational problems in real time: onboarding support, transaction monitoring, anomaly detection, duplicate suppression, certificate management, configuration correction, remediation orchestration, partner communications, operational analytics, and continuous optimization. Work is prioritised by severity, confidence, business impact, partner tier, and policy threshold — not ticket arrival order.

## Runbooks, rebuilt as code
Traditional runbooks sit in wikis and depend on human interpretation. Aiden transforms them into executable operational patterns. Agents use runbooks to identify known issues, determine the correct resolution path, perform checks in sequence, apply approved remediations, and validate outcomes. Operational knowledge stops being trapped in individuals. It becomes a reusable, governed asset that improves consistency, compresses response time, and reduces the dependency on manual intervention for known problems.

---

## The governed L1–L3 escalation model

**L1 — Autonomous execution**
Agents handle a large share of repetitive, low-complexity events: known transaction failures, standard retries, routing issues, duplicate events, predefined validation breaches. Runbook-based and inference-guided. Humans stay out of the loop by design, within policy.

**L2 — Human-supervised exceptions**
Operators supervise exceptions, review low-confidence decisions, validate non-standard remediation paths, and manage policy-sensitive events. The hub gives them every piece of relevant context from the knowledge graph in one place.

**L3 — Engineering and platform specialists**
Deep-rooted platform defects, architectural issues, unknown patterns, and design-level changes. L3 stops being a dumping ground for L1 problems and goes back to its actual job: resilience, platform improvement, future-state optimization.

Ticket volume at the lower tiers drops. Resolution speed improves. High-value technical resources focus on the work only they can do.

## The future-state operating model, in three layers

**1. Autonomous Execution Fabric**
Agents continuously monitor operations, execute straight-through remediation where confidence and policy allow, and keep transactions, mappings, and integrations running with minimal human intervention.

**2. Human Governance and Exception Control**
Operations teams define autonomy thresholds, manage escalations, supervise sensitive scenarios, enforce compliance, and improve agentic behavior over time. Humans govern the edges; they don't hand-triage the middle.

**3. Strategic Optimization and Business Enablement**
Leadership uses predictive operational intelligence to strengthen SLA strategy, improve ecosystem performance, accelerate partner operations, and make forward-looking business decisions — rather than spending their time in incident reviews.
