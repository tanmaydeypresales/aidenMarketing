The Problem

The modernisation programme that never moves

Every bank and insurer has one. It sits in the roadmap. It gets a budget. A team assembles. Workshops happen.

Then, 8–12 weeks in, the programme stalls. Not because the technology isn't ready. Not because the team isn't capable.

Because nobody can answer the most fundamental question: What is actually in this system — and what will break if we touch it?

Decades of business rules are embedded in legacy code, workflow engines, and integration layers. Nobody documented it. The architects who built it have moved on. The institutional memory is distributed across people who are one resignation away from gone.

So the migration decision gets deferred. Again.

Staying put feels conservative. It isn't. It's a compounding risk.
