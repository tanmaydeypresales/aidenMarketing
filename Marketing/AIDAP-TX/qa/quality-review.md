### Top Strengths

1. **Blueprint-first positioning is genuinely differentiated** — the angle "we understand your application before we automate it" has not been commoditized and is credible for enterprise buyers
2. **Pull-quote is memorable** — "AIDAP-TX doesn't just automate testing. It understands what needs to be tested — and why." will land in executive conversations
3. **Three-pillar structure maps cleanly to buyer segments** — Blueprint-First (for QA leaders), Full-Stack Testing (for engineering), Transformation-Ready (for program sponsors)
4. **Core capabilities list is scannable and complete** — no filler, no repetition
5. **Tone is executive throughout** — no startup-ish claims, no hype

### Risks Reviewed and Addressed

| Risk | Resolution |
|------|------------|
| "AI-powered" could sound generic | Anchored with "blueprint-first" specific differentiator — not just a label |
| Technical note at bottom might feel redundant | Intentional: provides depth for technical readers without cluttering main sections |
| No client proof points | Flagged for future version; copy is structured to add client evidence without restructuring |
| Transformation-Ready pillar could blend with differentiator section | Separated by purpose — pillar describes capability mode; differentiator section explains philosophy |
